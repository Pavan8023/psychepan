Train The Trainer

Empowering trainers to become exceptional through 'Train the Trainer' program

Our uniquely designed “Train the Trainer” has been powered and Enriched by NLP tools and techniques.to provide employees the abilities, information, and methods required to instruct others successfully. This training strategy recognises that in addition to subject-matter competence, trainers also need a certain set of facilitation and instructional abilities in order to effectively transmit information to their students. A “Train the Trainer” program’s main objective is to equip participants to become competent trainers on their own. This entails not only comprehending the material they will instruct, but also becoming an expert in a variety of training methods, communication approaches, and adult learning concepts. The main topics commonly addressed in a “Train the Trainer” programme are broken down as follows:

  • Learning and styles of learning: The best ways for adults to learn are discussed with participants, along with ideas like self-directed learning, relevance, and past experience. Trainers may design more efficient and interesting learning sessions by being aware of these concepts.
  • Training Needs Analysis : Identifying training needs for a program is essential to ensure that the training content and approach are aligned with the participants’ requirements and expectations.
  • Design of training programs: Our Trainers dive into the process of creating training materials and courses in the area of instructional design. This entails organising the information, establishing learning goals, and developing activities that encourage active learning
  • Delivering the Training Program: Information must be presented in a way that is clear, interesting, and confident. Trainers receive training in attention-grabbing strategies that include the use of narrative, visual aids, and powerful body language.
  • Development of Training Material: Our trainers dive into the process of making the employees understand their audience , and keeping in mind technological and cultural integration while developing their material
  • Facilitation Skills : Trainers study many methods for facilitating learning, such as interactive activities, role-playing, group discussions, and case studies. These methods promote comprehension and encourage involvement.
  • Communication Skills: The key to successful training is effective communication. Trainers acquire the skills necessary to communicate information coherently, engage audiences, pose insightful queries, and moderate debates.

HANDLING DIFFICULT CONVERSATION

Mastering the Art of Handling Difficult Dialogues

Psyche Panacea’s Training for Handling Difficult Conversation is intended to give staff members the knowledge and tools needed to handle difficult conversations skilfully and professionally. Giving criticism, addressing performance difficulties, settling disputes, breaking bad news, or broaching delicate subjects may all be part of these interactions. The following are some essential topics that are frequently taught in business training for conducting challenging conversations:

  • Active Listening: Developing the ability to actively listen can help you fully comprehend the worries and viewpoints of others. To ensure clear communication, this entails paying listening, paraphrasing, and clarifying information.
  • Emotional Intelligence: Improving emotional intelligence to recognise and control one’s own emotions during challenging interactions as well as those of others. Empathy, self-awareness, and self-control are included in this.
  • Planning and preparation: Teaching participants how to define clear goals, foresee probable obstacles, and prepare efficient communication tactics in order to prepare for challenging dialogues.
  • Constructive Feedback: Offering methods for giving constructive criticism in a kind and non-threatening way. This entails giving particular illustrations, concentrating on behaviours, and making recommendations for change.
  • Diffusing Defensiveness: Techniques for dealing with resistance and defensiveness in difficult conversations. This entails establishing a secure and judgment-free environment, paying attention intently, and reacting coolly.
  • Dealing with Strong Emotions: Techniques for controlling feelings during challenging interactions, such as maintaining composure, displaying proper body language, and recognising and validating feelings.
  • Role-playing and Practise: Involving participants in simulations and role-playing exercises to help them practise having challenging talks in a safe and encouraging setting. They can do this to improve their communication skills and get feedback.
  • Giving participants the tools, they need to handle conflict, including ways to recognise its common causes, communicate assertively, and reach win-win compromises.
  • Introduce the concepts of peaceful communication, which place an emphasis on speaking one’s truth while still having empathy for the needs and feelings of others.
  • Raising awareness of cultural variations and how they affect expectations and communication techniques. supplying rules for responsibly managing cross-cultural interactions and averting misunderstandings.
  • Discussing methods for taking further action and achieving resolution following unpleasant conversations is known as “follow-up and resolution.” This entails establishing objectives, keeping track of development, and preserving open lines of communication.
    • Workshops, simulations, case studies, role-playing activities, and coaching sessions are used to give corporate training on how to handle challenging conversations. It helps participants to equip with real-world methods, equipment, and tactics that they may use right away in the workplace.

MENTORING SKILLS

POSH (Prevention of Sexual Harassment)

Creating Respectful Workspaces: Preventing Sexual Harassment for a Safer Future.

Establishing a secure and respectful workplace that is free from all forms of sexual harassment requires corporate training in prevention of sexual harassment (POSH). Employees will learn about the idea of sexual harassment, how it affects people and the organisation, and how to prevent and deal with such events. The following are significant topics that POSH corporate training often covers:

  • Understanding Sexual Harassment: Outlining what sexual harassment is and what constitutes unprofessional conduct in the workplace. recognising the difference between overt and covert harassment.
  • Legal System and Guidelines: Employee education about sexual harassment laws and regulations, including local legislation and the organization’s own policies and procedures. describing the effects of not complying.
  • Creating Awareness:Spreading knowledge about how sexual harassment affects victims, the workplace, and total output. teaching workers the value of a courteous, inclusive workplace atmosphere.
  • Privacy and Confidentiality: Stressing the significance of upholding privacy and confidentiality throughout the reporting and inquiry process. ensuring that staff members are aware of their legal rights and the organization’s dedication to upholding those rights.
  • Encouraging Respectful Communication: Promoting respectful interactions at work by teaching good communication techniques. putting a focus on the value of consent, limits, and cultural awareness in business partnerships.
  • Procedures for Investigation and Disciplinary Action: Outlining the steps taken to investigate claims of sexual harassment and the potential sanctions that may be imposed on offenders
  • Regular Refresher Training: Recognising that POSH training should be a continuous process, regularly informing staff on any changes to laws or regulations and reinforcing the important principles through refresher training sessions.
  • Identifying many forms of sexual harassment, including verbal, nonverbal, visual, and physical harassment. Using case studies and examples to assist staff in identifying and reporting issues.
  • Informing staff members of the reporting processes and resources available for sexual harassment events. urging them to report immediately and without worry of punishment.
  • Encourage employees to engage as active bystanders and take action when they see sexual harassment taking place. presenting methods and plans for securely intervening and aiding the victim.
  • Offering techniques and resources to stop sexual harassment, such as advancing gender equality, enforcing a zero-tolerance rule, and cultivating an inclusive and respectful culture.
    • Delivering POSH training by knowledgeable instructors with experience who are sensitive to the needs and concerns of participants is crucial. The instruction ought to be participatory, allowing for queries, conversations, and experience sharing.
    Organisations should promote a respectful and safe work environment, reduce the incidence of sexual harassment, and make sure that all employees are informed of their rights and obligations by offering thorough POSH training.

GENDER SENSITIZATION

Promoting Understanding: Embracing Gender Sensitization for Inclusive Progress

Psyche Panacea’s GENDER SENSITIZATION program, sometimes referred to as gender awareness training or gender sensitivity training, aims to increase understanding of gender-related issues, stereotypes, prejudices, and discrimination. This programme aims to promote an inclusive and equitable workplace where everyone feels appreciated, valued, and treated fairly regardless of their gender identification. The following significant topics are frequently covered in gender sensitization training:

  • Gender-Based Discrimination and Harassment: Informing participants on the various types of gender-based discrimination, such as sexual harassment, and offering advice on how to stop, report, and handle such incidents.
  • Organisational Policies and Practises: Examining current guidelines for diversity, inclusion, and gender equality. identifying areas for development and talking about how to make policies and practises more inclusive.
  • Building Support: Motivating participants to support and act as allies for gender equality. looking at ways to empower people and build a network of support to handle gender-related difficulties at work.
  • Case Studies and Group Discussions: Case studies, scenarios, and group discussions are used to promote learning, the sharing of personal experiences, and conversation about the opportunities and problems specific to gender.
  • Investigating the impact of society and cultural norms on gender stereotypes and prejudices is key to understanding them. demonstrating how these prejudices might restrict opportunity and support discrimination.
  • Raising awareness of unconscious biases and how they may affect communications, professional interactions, and decision-making. assisting participants in recognising and overcoming their own biases.
  • Promoting the use of inclusive language and considerate communication that recognises and respects the variety of gender identities and expressions. discussing the use of pronouns and other issues.
  • Realising how gender and other social identities, such as race, ethnicity, disability, and socioeconomic status, are intertwined. recognising the particular difficulties that people who have numerous identities encounter.
    • The training encourages open discussions and sharing of viewpoints, it is crucial to include participants through interactive techniques, offer real-world examples, and make sure the training is applicable to the context of their workplaces. Our training is tailor-made to suit a company’s need and requirement, in accordance with the varied workplace culture, the training offers an opportunity for all employees to grow and contribute to their full potential by implementing gender sensitization training.

Workplace/Mental Health Counselling

Nurturing growth and well-being through tailored workspace counseling.

Workplace counselling is a proactive strategy, confidential, and supportive service offered within an organisation to assist workers in addressing personal, emotional, and psychological difficulties that might affect their well-being, productivity, and general sense of satisfaction. It involves qualified therapists or counsellors who provide a private, judgment-free setting for staff members to voice their worries and get advice. Workplace counselling seeks to enhance productivity, foster mental wellness, and foster a more positive work atmosphere.
